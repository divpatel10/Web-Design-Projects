Carl Sagan

The man who took us on a voyage through the Cosmos

Here's a time line of Carl Sagan's Life

  • 1934: Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1956: Earned his Master's Degree in Physics
  • 1960: Earned his doctorate in Astronomy and Astrophysics. His Dissertation was titled 'Physical Studies of Planets'
  • 1968: Joined Cornell University as an associate Professor, and in 1970, he became a full professort and the Director fo the laborarotry for Planetary Studies at the University.
  • 1971: Worked with Frank Drake, to design the infamous Pioneer's Plagues, a message aimed at extraterrestrial life.
  • 1975: Worked with NASA where he helped select the Mars landing site for the Viking probes
  • 1976: he became the David Duncan Professor of Astronomy and Space Sciences, a position he held for the remainder of his life. Concurrently, he also continued his association with NASA and co-designed Voyager Golden Record.
  • 1977: Published his book "The Dragons of Eden: Speculations on the Evolution of Human Intelligence" which later won the Pulitzer price.
  • 1980: Published his new book "Cosmos" for which he is best known for. In the same year, the book was turned into a thirteen part television series called ‘Cosmos: A Personal Voyage’.
  • 1985: Published his first science fiction novel "Contact" which deals with the theme of contact between humanity and a more technologically advanced, extraterrestrial life form.
  • 1992: Died at the age of 62, on December 20,1996
